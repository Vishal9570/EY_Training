Multi-agent
1. Distinct Knowledge Domains

Each stage requires specialized expertise:

Stage					Domain
CT Scan Interpretation		Radiology
Medication Cross-Check		Clinical Decision Support / Pharmacology
Follow-up Recommendation	Care Pathway Management
Appointment Booking		EMR Operations
GP Letter & Patient Summary	Medical Communication

The process is not just sequential—it requires approval checkpoints.

CT Scan
   ↓
Radiology Report
   ↓ (validation gate)
Contraindication Check
   ↓ (validation gate)
Follow-up Recommendation
   ↓ (validation gate)
EMR Scheduling
   ↓
Patient Communication

3. Different Tool Access / Security Boundaries

Radiology agent:

PACS system
Imaging models

Clinical CDS agent:

Medication history
Drug interaction databases

Scheduling agent:

EMR scheduling APIs

Communication agent:

Patient communication templates
GP letter generation

Healthcare systems typically follow the principle of least privilege, making separate agents preferable.

4. Failure Isolation

Suppose:

Radiology report succeeds
Medication checker detects contraindication

The workflow should stop immediately and escalate to a clinician.

Multi-agent systems naturally support:

Human-in-the-loop reviews
Escalation workflows
Audit logging
Regulatory compliance
