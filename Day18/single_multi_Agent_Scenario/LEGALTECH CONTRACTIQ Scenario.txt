I would choose a Multi-Agent Architecture because the workload naturally separates into two phases: 
a highly parallel contract-extraction stage and a cross-document synthesis stage. 
The 800 contracts are independent during extraction, making them ideal for parallel processing by multiple extraction 
agents, while later stages require specialized agents for regulatory review, dependency analysis, 
and executive risk reporting. A single-agent architecture would struggle to meet the 4-hour SLA due to sequential 
processing and context limitations. 
A Map-Reduce style multi-agent architecture provides scalability, fault isolation, and efficient cross-document reasoning.
