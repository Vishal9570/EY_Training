The latency variation is mostly caused by the "Claude generate" stage, not by embedding or retrieval.

Root cause of latency variation:

LLM generation is the biggest contributor:

For queries like Q3, Q6, Q7, the green bar is very large, which means the model spent much more time generating the answer.


This usually happens when:

the response is longer
the reasoning is more complex


BLUE:

BLEU measures word-level precision, BLEU focuses on exact phrase overlap, not depth or grounding.

BLEU is less useful for explaining the latency variation because BLEU measures exact wording overlap with a reference answer. 
A slower response does not necessarily produce wording closer to the reference.



ROUGE:

Queries with higher latency may have better ROUGE scores because the model is covering more relevant information from the retrieved documents.

ROUGE is more relevant, because longer generation often means the model is including more of the important content from retrieved documents, leading to better content coverage. I


Faithfulness:

because the model is not just replying quickly it is generating a more evidence-based answer.

Faithfulness is the most relevant metric here, because increased generation time may indicate that the model is using retrieved evidence more 
carefully and producing a more grounded, less hallucinated answer.



therefore:

The main cause of latency variation is response generation complexity, and this variation is better interpreted through ROUGE and faithfulness
 rather than BLEU.

One-line answer:

The latency variation is mainly driven by the generation stage, and in quality terms it is more closely related to 
ROUGE (content coverage) and faithfulness (groundedness) than to BLEU (exact wording match).
